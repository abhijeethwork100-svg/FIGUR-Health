What is Mounjaro & how does it work?

The only drug in its class, Mounjaro, is a dual-action drug that activates two hormones in the gut: GLP-1 and GIP. It is approved for weight loss treatment in India and is available as a once-weekly injection.

When injected correctly, Mounjaro does the following:

  • Control appetite
  • Reduce food cravings
  • Help manage calorie intake
  • Curb blood sugar levels

Because this mechanism affects multiple organs, the body often needs time to adapt to it.

That’s why your doctor will get you started on the lowest Mounjaro dose and gradually increase the dosage over the next few weeks.

This helps you adjust gently to the weight loss medicine with reduced side effects.

Mounjaro dosage chart

Doctors prescribe Mounjaro at the lowest, safest dose, i.e., 2.5 mg. The doses are usually increased every four weeks.

About your dosages:

  • 2.5 mg dose: Starting dosage to help your body adjust to the medicine.
  • 5 mg dose: Standard maintenance dosage for most patients.
  • 7.5 mg to 15 mg dose: Additional dosages offered for further weight loss support.

How do doctors decide which dose is right for you?

Everyone’s weight loss journey is different — and so is your Mounjaro dose. Your doctor will start you on a small dose and slowly adjust it depending on how your body reacts.

They’ll look at things like:

  • Your goals: Some people do well on lower doses, others need a gradual increase for steady results.
  • Your progress: Are you losing weight comfortably?
  • Side effects: Any nausea or bloating that needs care?
  • Your health: If you have any liver or kidney concerns, your doctor may go slower.
  • Health reports: Are your blood pressure, thyroid levels, or blood sugar levels normal?

There’s no rush. The goal is to find the dose that suits your body best, helps you lose weight safely, and keeps you feeling well throughout the journey.

How to monitor your response to dose changes?

One of the best and most useful ways to understand how your body responds to changes, be it medication, diet, or exercise, is tracking.

You can simply enter your daily glucose readings, weight, and any side effects experienced in a diary. If fitness apps work for you, give them a go!

A written record of your response to dose changes can also help your doctor monitor your health.

This way, you play a more proactive role in taking charge of your health and maximising your doctor appointments.

What to do if you miss a dose?

When you cannot take your dose within four days of the scheduled injection, it is counted as a missed dose. However, there is no need to panic if this happens. Here’s what you can do:

  • If it’s been less than four days (96 hours) since your scheduled injection, you can still take the missed dose.
  • If more than four days have passed, skip that week’s dose and continue with your usual schedule the following week.
  • Never take two doses at once or try to “catch up” by injecting more.

Mounjaro Dosage Schedule :

You can take one dose on any day shown in the ‘missed dose’ row. If it’s a day, skip the dose and wait for your next regular dosing day.

Important:

To change the day of taking the medication, keep a gap of at least three full days, or 72 hours, between the two doses.

When should you pause or lower your dose?

Sometimes, the body needs more time to adjust to a particular dose of Mounjaro. Certain situations may warrant you to lower your dose or pause it altogether:

  • If the side effects are too severe or persistent.
  • If you get pregnant while on Mounjaro.
  • If you have issues with your liver or kidneys.
  • If you have trouble tolerating a higher dose.
  • If you experience low blood sugar (hypoglycemia), especially if you are on other anti-diabetes medications too.

Always keep your doctor in the loop. Even small updates can help them support you better.

How to ensure a smoother dose escalation?

Adapting to any new medication, especially one that affects your appetite and glucose metabolism, takes some time. But with these small changes, you can adapt to the new dose better:

  • Eat light and chew slowly during the first few days.
  • Drink enough water throughout the day.
  • You can try light activities like walking, yoga, or gentle stretching to keep nausea in check.
  • Always keep emergency contacts readily accessible in case of an allergic reaction.

Go at your own pace, but with proper medical supervision

The Mounjaro dosage chart has been carefully designed while keeping your safety, comfort, and health goals as the top priority. Some people see results early, while others take a little longer.

And that’s okay.

You just need to be consistent and patient and work closely with your doctor to find the dose that works best for you.

FAQs

  • What is the best time of the day to take Mounjaro?

    The good thing about taking Mounjaro is that you don’t have to worry about taking it before or after meals. This doesn’t affect how well it works.

    The key is to choose a time that’s easiest and most convenient for you to remember. This helps you stay consistent with your doses and stick to your schedule. So to make things easier, take it on the same day and at the same time every week.

  • What if I accidentally take the wrong dose?

    If you take a higher dose than prescribed, contact your doctor immediately. Mild overdose may cause nausea or vomiting, but a severe overdose requires urgent medical attention.

  • Can I split my weekly dose into smaller doses?

    No. Mounjaro is designed and prescribed to be taken once a week only. Splitting the dose can reduce its effectiveness and increase the chances of side effects.

  • Does the dose affect how fast I lose weight?

    Not always. Weight loss depends on how your body responds. Some people lose more weight on lower doses, especially when combined with lifestyle changes.

  • Will increasing my dose reduce my hunger even more?

    Yes. Increasing the dose may increase the suppression of appetite. This is because GLP-1 and GIP activity is increased, which sends stronger ‘fullness signals’ to the brain.

  • Can I drink alcohol while taking Mounjaro?

    You can take alcohol while on Mounjaro, but only in moderation. Heavy drinking can aggravate the side effects and pose a risk to your overall health. Consult your doctor to know more.

  • Can I inject Mounjaro at a different time each week?

    Ideally, it is preferable to take Mounjaro on the same day and time each week.

    But if you do need to change your injection day or time, maintain a gap of at least 72 hours from the previous dose.

  • How do I know if the medication is working?

    Monitor your weight, appetite, and energy to understand if Mounjaro is actually working for you.

    If the medicine is working, you will experience less cravings, more energy, and eventually, weight loss. Your blood pressure and blood sugar levels will stabilise.

  • How long should I stay on Mounjaro?

    The length of treatment with Mounjaro depends on your health goals. You may need to take this medication anywhere from six months to several years.

    For example, people with type 2 diabetes may need to take Mounjaro long-term to manage blood sugar levels.

    For weight loss and maintenance, your doctor will guide you on when and how to stop taking Moujaro gradually and safely.

  • Can I stop Mounjaro for a month and restart where I left off?

    When it comes to any medication, especially Mounjaro, it’s best to consult your doctor before stopping or restarting it after a break.

    You may need to restart at a lower dose, like 2.5 mg, rather than resuming your previous dose. This is because your body’s tolerance to the medication may decrease during the break. Jumping back to a higher dose could increase the risk of side effects.

    Your doctor will evaluate your current health status, treatment goals, and any changes in your condition and decide the safest way to resume Mounjaro.

  • How do I administer my dose correctly?

    For Mounjaro to work properly, it’s important to inject it safely and hygienically.

    Check out this step-by-step guide on how to inject Mounjaro properly so you can get the most out of it with fewer chances of post-injection complications.

  • How do I manage Mounjaro side effects?

    Some people experience side effects in the first few weeks of taking Mounjaro. These effects are seen as a result of increasing the dose.

    The milder effects, like nausea, diarrhoea, vomiting, etc., are usually temporary and get better with time.

    If you wish to manage Mounjaro side effects, here are some things you can try:

    • Start with a low dose and increase it slowly.
    • Eat small, bland meals and avoid fatty, spicy, or greasy foods.
    • Drink plenty of water or electrolytes.
    • Take Mounjaro with food if your doctor advises you to.
    • Avoid drinking caffeine, alcohol, and fruit juices if you have diarrhoea.
    • Rest whenever you feel unwell.
    • Reach out to your doctor immediately if you have severe pain, persistent vomiting, symptoms of low blood sugar, allergic reactions, or swelling.
