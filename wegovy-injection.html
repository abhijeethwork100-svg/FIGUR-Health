How does Wegovy work?

Wegovy is made of semaglutide, which is a GLP-1 receptor agonist. Semaglutide mimics the GLP-1 hormone in the body and does the following:

Releases insulin

The GLP-1 hormone signals the pancreas to release insulin when blood sugar levels rise. This moves sugar into the cells to generate energy.

Slows digestion

The hormone slows down the pace of food movement through the stomach. This results in feeling fuller for longer after eating.

Reduces sugar production in the liver

The hormone decreases sugar production in the liver when it is not required.

Controls appetite

GLP-1 also affects the areas of the brain that control appetite. As a result, you end up feeling less hungry.

All these actions eventually lead to gradual weight loss.

Are you eligible to take Wegovy?

Although Wegovy helps people effortlessly lose weight, can any obese or overweight person take the weight loss injection in India?

Wegovy is a prescription-only weight loss injection.

This means you need a doctor‚Äôs prescription to take it for weight loss. This also means that your doctor should find it reasonable to prescribe Wegovy to you.

You are eligible for Wegovy if you have:

A BMI of:

> 30 kg/m2

> 27 kg/m2 with at least one weight-related medical condition, such as:

  • Prediabetes
  • Type 2 diabetes
  • High blood pressure
  • History of heart attack or stroke
  • You have type 2 diabetes, and all other forms of medication have failed to manage it

You might not be eligible to take Wegovy if you have:

  • Type 1 diabetes
  • Pancreatitis (inflammation in the pancreas)
  • Personal or family history of Medullary Thyroid Carcinoma (MTC), a type of thyroid cancer
  • Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia Syndrome Type 2 (MEN 2)
  • Allergy to Wegovy or any of the ingredients present in Wegovy

Inform your doctor if any or all of the following apply to you before taking Wegovy:

  • You are taking birth control pills
  • You have severe problems with your stomach or kidneys, such as gastroparesis (slowed emptying of the stomach)
  • You are taking other diabetic medicines, such as sulfonylureas or insulin
  • You have a history of diabetic retinopathy (vision issues due to diabetes)
  • You are scheduled for surgery that involves anaesthesia or sleep medicines
  • You are pregnant or planning to become pregnant
  • You are breastfeeding or planning to breastfeed

How to take Wegovy

Taking the Wegovy injection for weight loss is quite an easy process.

Wegovy is available as FlexTouch, a pre-filled injection pen.

What‚Äôs inside a Wegovy package?

Inside a Wegovy package, you get:

  • 1 pre-filled injection pen
  • 4 disposable needles

Wegovy is designed to be taken once a week. It is to be taken under the skin (subcutaneously).

Ideal injection sites for Wegovy:

  • Stomach (2 inches away from the belly button)
  • Upper thighs
  • Upper arms

Pro tip:

You can inject Wegovy in the same body part each week, but ensure that you rotate the areas on that particular body part to avoid irritation.

It is advised that you take Wegovy on the same day, at the same time every week.

Wegovy can be self-injected. For first-time users, a healthcare professional can demonstrate how to inject Wegovy.

Does Wegovy have any side effects?

Every medicine comes with a few side effects. Wegovy is no exception.

Some of the most common Wegovy side effects (occur in more than 1 of 10 people) include:

  • Headache
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Diarrhoea
  • Constipation
  • Stomach pain
  • Fatigue

Common side effects (affecting 1 in 10 people) include:

  • Feeling dizzy
  • Upset stomach
  • Burping
  • Gas
  • Bloating in the stomach
  • Gastritis (inflamed stomach)
  • Acid reflux or heartburn
  • Hair loss
  • Injection site reactions
  • Change in taste
  • Low blood sugar (hypoglycemia) in diabetic patients

Wegovy also has some serious but rare side effects. These include:

  • Pancreatitis (inflammation of the pancreas)
  • Gallbladder problems
  • Hypoglycemia (low blood sugar)
  • Kidney failure
  • Vision change in type 2 diabetics
  • Increased heart rate
  • Depression/suicidal thoughts
  • Allergic reactions (swelling of face, nose, lips)
  • Rash
  • Difficulty swallowing/breathing

These side effects usually subside within a few days to a few weeks.
