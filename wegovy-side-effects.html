To understand Wegovy‚Äôs side effects better, we first need to know what exactly happens when we take the medicine.

Wegovy works by mimicking the actions of a hormone in your body called GLP-1 (glucagon-like peptide-1).

  • Controls appetite:
    Wegovy targets the parts of your brain that control hunger. This reduces food cravings and makes it easier to eat less without feeling too hungry.
  • Keeps you full for longer:
    It slows how quickly food leaves your stomach. As a result, meals keep you satisfied for longer, which naturally reduces the urge to overeat.
  • Supports blood sugar control:
    After meals, Wegovy helps your body release more insulin and lowers glucagon levels in the blood.

This helps you:

  • Stay full even with smaller meals.
  • Feel less hungry between meals.
  • Cut down on cravings.
  • Manage blood sugar more effectively.
  • Lose weight steadily over time.

Why does Wegovy cause side effects?

The reason why people experience side effects while on Wegovy is that the body is adjusting to new changes.

GLP-1 receptors are present in the gut, brain, pancreas, heart, kidneys, and blood vessels. When these receptors are activated by Wegovy, they affect how your body digests food, controls hunger, and regulates insulin.

That‚Äôs why symptoms like nausea, bloating, or tiredness are common in the beginning. They are signs that your body is shifting gears.

What are the side effects of taking Wegovy?

Like any medicine, Wegovy can cause side effects. But not everyone gets them, and most are mild and temporary.

Common side effects:

Uncommon side effects:

Do the side effects go away over time?

Yes, most Wegovy side effects do get better with time. Symptoms like nausea, tiredness, headache, constipation, or diarrhoea are common in the initial days of increasing your dose.

  • Nausea often takes 7-8 days to settle.
  • Diarrhoea usually lasts 3 days.
  • Vomiting gets better after 2 days.
  • Constipation can take about 47 days on average to go away.
Important:

Wegovy side effects are temporary and improve as your body adjusts. In case the side effects persist or worsen over time, please get in touch with your doctor.

How to manage Wegovy side effects?

Usually, doctors prescribe the lowest dose first and increase gradually. This is to give your body some time to get used to the new medication.

These simple and easy-to-follow tips will help you manage some common Wegovy side effects better:

How to manage Wegovy side effects?

  • For nausea:

    • Eat several small meals instead of a couple of large ones.
    • Start your meals by eating thin dal or clear soup first.
    • Avoid masala and oil in your food for some time.
    • Eat mindfully.
    • Stay hydrated with adrak chai, pudina tea, jeera water, or nimbu paani.
    • Add hing in dals for better digestion.

  • For vomiting:

    • Drink ORS or sweet lemon water.
    • Have light meals like khichdi or curd rice.
    • Avoid lying down immediately after meals.
    • Take small but frequent sips of water.
    • Try taking Wegovy before going to bed to sleep through symptoms.

  • For diarrhoea:

    • Rehydrate with nimbu paani, nariyal paani, or lassi.
    • Eat apples, bananas, or curd rice.
    • Consume isabgol (psyllium husk) if your doctor permits.

  • For constipation:

    • Drink at least 8‚Äì10 glasses of water daily.
    • Try triphala churna if your doctor permits.
    • Consume soaked methi seeds.
    • Have more whole vegetables, whole grains, and papaya.

  • For decreased appetite:

    • Eat protein-rich foods like dal, paneer, soya, etc.
    • Start taking multivitamins if your doctor permits.
    • Eat small meals and do not force-feed yourself.

  • For fatigue:

    • Stay well-hydrated.
    • Eat nutritious grains such as ragi, jowar, sattu, etc.
    • Consume iron-rich foods like spinach and beetroot.

  • For headaches:

    • Drink 8-10 glasses of water every day.
    • Sleep for at least 8 hours a day.
    • Sleep at the same time each day.
    • Use over-the-counter pain relievers if needed.
    • Drink tulsi or chamomile tea for relief.

  • For injection site reactions:

    • Rotate the injection sites every time.
    • Use a cold compress if there's swelling or discomfort at the site.

Why Wegovy affects different people in different ways?

You might feel, ‚ÄúWhy does Wegovy affect me differently than others?‚Äù

You're not imagining it. Sensitivity to a particular medication can be different for everyone.

Some reasons include:

  • You may have medical conditions related to the kidneys, eyes, or thyroid gland, which may affect your body‚Äôs ability to process Wegovy.
  • You have a sensitive gut (perhaps because of IBS, IBD, Crohn's, etc.)
  • Age and metabolism.
  • Taking insulin or other medications like antidiabetics, anti-hypertensives, antidepressants, etc.
  • Genetics
Remember:

If you feel overwhelmed, talk to your doctor. You might need a slower dose increase, or simply more time to adjust.

When should you visit the doctor?

Certain conditions or symptoms while on Wegovy may warrant you to visit your doctor as early as possible:

  • A lump in your neck, difficulty swallowing, hoarseness, or shortness of breath.
  • Severe or ongoing stomach pain, with or without vomiting.
  • Feeling lightheaded, weak, or dehydrated due to nausea, vomiting, or diarrhoea.
  • Yellowing of your skin or eyes, dark urine, or pale stools.
  • The heartbeat feels fast or irregular while at rest.
  • Low blood sugar symptoms such as shakiness, confusion, or sweating.
  • Changes in vision, especially if you have diabetes.
  • Feeling unusually tired, dizzy, or mentally unwell.
  • Swelling, breathing difficulty, or skin reactions after an injection.
  • Difficulty urinating, lower abdominal pain, or notice a change in urine colour.
  • Bloating or constipation that does not improve.

FAQs

  • What medications should you not take with Wegovy?

    A drug interaction happens when the presence of one drug affects the actions of the second drug. Wegovy, too, can interact with different medications and supplements.

    Some examples include painkillers (NSAIDs), certain antibiotics, antidepressants, and some blood pressure medications.

    Also, combining Wegovy with insulin, oral diabetes medications and other GLP-1 (or GIP) agents can cause undesirable effects. Always inform your doctor if you are taking any medications.

  • Does Wegovy cause hair loss?

    Although there is no direct link between hair loss and Wegovy, some people do notice more hair fall after starting on the drug. In a clinical trial[1] involving around 1961 people over 68 weeks, around 2.5 % participants reported hair loss.

    That said, most participants recovered while still taking the weight loss treatment with Wegovy.

  • What happens if you eat too much food on Wegovy?

    Wegovy slows down how quickly your stomach empties, so you'll feel full much sooner than usual. So eating past that new 'full point' will increase the chances of gastrointestinal side effects like:

    • Nausea
    • Bloating or stomach discomfort
    • Acid reflux
    • Vomiting, especially during dose escalation

    Overeating on Wegovy isn‚Äôt dangerous in most cases, but it can be really uncomfortable. If it happens, you may need to eat simple, light meals for some time and visit your doctor.

  • Which one is safer? Wegovy or Mounjaro?

    Both Wegovy and Mounjaro are considered safe when used as prescribed, but they work slightly differently and may suit different people.

    Wegovy contains semaglutide, while Mounjaro contains tirzepatide, which targets both GLP-1 and GIP hormones.

    Because of this, Mounjaro may cause more gastrointestinal side effects in some users. But it may also offer greater weight loss for others.

    As for safety, both come with similar risks:

    • Nausea, vomiting, constipation
    • Possible risk of pancreatitis
    • A thyroid warning based on animal studies.

  • Can Wegovy cause Ozempic face?

    'Ozempic face' refers to the tired or hollow look some people get after losing weight quickly. It manifests in the form of sunken cheeks, looser skin, or more visible wrinkles. It happens because of quick fat loss throughout the body, and especially from the face.

    Wegovy, like other GLP-1 meds, can lead to similar changes if weight loss happens too fast. Losing weight gradually while focusing on your diet and exercise can help minimise this.

  • Can I take a break from Wegovy?

    It‚Äôs best not to skip doses of Wegovy on purpose. This medication works best when it‚Äôs taken consistently every week. Missing a dose can reduce appetite control and bring back side effects when you restart.

    If you miss a dose:

    • Take it within five days.
    • If it‚Äôs been more than 5 days, skip it and take the next one as scheduled.
    • Don‚Äôt double-dose.

    If you have missed more than one dose, you should check in with your doctor. They may ask you to go back to a lower dose so you adjust easily.

  • Can Wegovy cause heart attacks?

    There‚Äôs no direct evidence that Wegovy causes heart attacks in healthy users. In fact, GLP-1 medications like Wegovy have been shown to reduce the risk of heart disease in people with type 2 diabetes.

    However, if you already have a heart condition, your doctor will want to monitor you more closely, especially when you are adjusting to Wegovy during your initial days.

  • Why am I still so hungry on Wegovy?

    Wegovy reduces appetite for most people, but not everyone feels that effect right away. If you‚Äôre still hungry:

    • Your dose may still be too low (especially in the first few weeks).
    • You might be confusing emotional hunger or habit-driven eating with real hunger.
    • Skipping meals or not eating enough protein and fibre can also make you feel hungrier.

  • Who should not use Wegovy?

    Wegovy is not recommended for:

    • People with a personal or family history of medullary thyroid cancer.
    • Those with multiple endocrine neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2).
    • Women who are pregnant or trying to conceive.
    • Women who are currently breastfeeding.
    • Anyone allergic to semaglutide or other ingredients in the injection.

    Always talk to your doctor before starting Wegovy to make sure it‚Äôs a safe fit.

  • Is it safe to take Wegovy for life?

    Wegovy is generally considered safe for long-term use, but ongoing studies are still tracking its effects beyond 2‚Äì3 years.

    However, like any medication, there are potential risks associated with prolonged use. Possible long-term side effects include pancreatitis, gallbladder disease (such as gallstones or inflammation of the gallbladder), and kidney damage.

    If it helps you manage your weight and improve health markers like blood sugar, cholesterol, and blood pressure, your doctor may recommend staying on it long-term and even monitor your health while you are on Wegovy.

  • What happens if you stop Wegovy?

    You may also see a reduction in the side effects from the medication, usually within the first week of stopping.

    Although stopping Wegovy is not harmful, it comes with a few changes, like:

    • Return of appetite to pre-treatment levels.
    • Increased food cravings.
    • Weight regain (if a healthy diet, exercise, and lifestyle changes are not followed).
    • Feel fatigued than usual.
    • Increased blood sugar levels.
    • Changes in mood (disappointment, sadness).

    If you're thinking of pausing or quitting Wegovy, don‚Äôt do it abruptly. Discuss this with your doctor. They may suggest tapering the dose or suggest alternatives that work better for you.

  • Can Wegovy cause depression?

    Although rare, Wegovy might cause or worsen depression and mood changes in some people.

    Recent studies [2][3] and adverse event reports have linked semaglutide to higher rates of depression, anxiety, and suicidal thoughts, especially at higher doses or in those with a history of mental health issues.

    But in Indian trials or safety monitoring, no statistically significant psychiatric side effects have been reported.

    That said, this aspect needs more research. Still, regulatory agencies advise monitoring mental health during treatment, particularly if you or your family has a history of mental health issues.

    If you notice new or worsening mood symptoms while on Wegovy, it‚Äôs important to speak to your doctor.
