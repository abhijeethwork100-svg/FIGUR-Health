What is Wegovy?

Wegovy is a once-weekly injection used for weight loss and weight management. Its active ingredient is semaglutide, a GLP-1 mimicking ingredient that the FDA approved in 2021. Novo Nordisk, a Danish pharmaceutical manufacturer, manufactures Wegovy. Apart from Wegovy, Novo Nordisk also manufactures the anti-diabetic drugs Ozempic and Rybelsus, which are made from semaglutide, the same active ingredient as Wegovy.

How does Wegovy tackle obesity?

Wegovy’s active ingredient, semaglutide, works by mimicking a hormone in your body called GLP-1 (glucagon-like peptide-1). This is what GLP-1 hormone does in your body:

How does GLP-1 work?
Releases insulin

GLP-1 hormone signals the pancreas to release insulin when blood sugar is high. It helps move sugar into cells for energy.

Slows digestion

The hormone slows down the movement of food through your stomach, making you feel full for longer after eating.

Reduces sugar production in the liver

The hormone decreases the amount of sugar a liver produces when it is not required.

Controls appetite

GLP-1 affects the areas of the brain that control appetite. As a result, you feel less hungry.

As a result, by imitating GLP-1, Wegovy helps you eat less and feel full, which reduces calories and helps you lose weight.

Does Wegovy really work for weight loss?

Yes, it does.

A 68-week study was conducted with 1961 participants. These participants were either obese or overweight with at least one obesity-related medical condition.

Some participants were administered a Wegovy injection along with a reduced-calorie diet and exercise, while others were given a placebo along with diet and exercise.

The average starting weight of both groups was 105 kgs.

The results at the end of the study were as follows:

  • 83% of participants lost 5% or more weight on Wegovy. In contrast, only 31% of participants lost the same amount of weight on a placebo.
  • 66% of participants lost 10% or more weight on Wegovy. In contrast, only 12% of participants lost the same weight on a placebo.
  • 48% of participants lost 15% or more weight on Wegovy. In contrast, only 5% of participants lost the same amount of weight on a placebo.

    • A separate 2-year study was conducted with 304 obese or overweight participants who had at least one weight-related medical condition.

How does Wegovy prevent cardiovascular events?

Wegovy received regulatory approval from the MHRA in 2021 for weight loss treatment. In 2024, the weight loss injection was also approved to prevent cardiovascular events in overweight or obese people.

A study was conducted with 17,604 participants to assess how Wegovy can benefit cardiovascular health in users. The participants were all above 45 years of age and had a BMI of more than 27. They were divided into two groups: Group A was given Wegovy and Group B was given placebo.

At the end of the study, the following was observed:

  • 6.5% of participants taking Wegovy experienced overall heart problems, compared to 8% of participants taking a placebo.
  • 2.7% of participants taking Wegovy experienced a heart attack, compared to 3.7% of participants taking a placebo.
  • 3.4% of participants taking Wegovy had to be hospitalised for heart problems, compared to 4.1% of participants taking a placebo.
  • 2.5% of participants taking Wegovy had a cardiovascular event-related death, compared to 3% of participants taking a placebo.

Who can take Wegovy?

If you want to take Wegovy for weight loss, you must meet at least one of the factors mentioned below:

  • Age: 18 or older.
  • Overweight or obese adults with a BMI of:
    • > 30 kg/m2
    • > 27 kg/m2 if one or more weight-related complications are present

Who cannot take Wegovy?

Wegovy is not an ideal weight loss injection for everyone. You might not be able to use Wegovy if you have one or more of the following:

  • A personal or family history of Medullary Thyroid Carcinoma (MTC).
  • Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia Syndrome Type 2 (MEN 2).
  • Pregnancy or breastfeeding.
  • A severe allergic reaction to semaglutide or any other compounds present in Wegovy.

Taking Wegovy: One step at a time

Injections may look daunting. But that doesn’t mean it is tough to inject yourself with Wegovy.

When to take Wegovy?

Wegovy is a once-weekly injection for weight loss. This means, you need to inject Wegovy only once a week, on the same day every week.

Usually, the timing of the injection doesn’t matter. However, taking the injection at the same day, at the same time every week will help you stay consistent - and that is the key to safer, faster weight loss.

How to take Wegovy?

Weight loss injections like Wegovy need to be administered carefully. Luckily, the injection pen is quite easy to inject.

How to inject Wegovy: Explained in 5 minutes

Check out this short and simple 5-minute video illustrating how to inject yourself with Wegovy.


1. Prepare your injection

Get the following supplies before you inject yourself:

  • Your Wegovy pen
  • One alcohol swab/soap and water
  • One gauze pad/cotton ball
  • One sharps bin for used Wegovy pens

Wash your hands well.

Check your Wegovy pen. Do not use it if it is:

  • Used or appears to have been used.
  • Broken or appears to have been broken.
  • Not reflecting clear or colourless medication.
  • Past expiry date.

2. Choose an injection site

  • Your doctor will help you choose the best injection site for you.
  • You can inject Wegovy into your upper legs, lower stomach, or upper arm
  • Do not inject in an area that is sensitive, bruised, red, or hard and has scars or stretch marks.
  • You can inject in the same area every week. But ensure that you switch spots.

3. Remove the pen cap

  • Pull the pen cap from your pen.

4. Injecting Wegovy

Push the pen firmly against your skin.

  • Continue applying pressure until the yellow bar stops moving.
  • If the yellow bar doesn’t start to move, press the pen more firmly against your skin.

You will hear two clicks when you inject

  • 1st click: This means that the injection has started.
  • 2nd click: This means that the injection is ongoing.

If you see blood at the injection site, press lightly with a gauze pad or a cotton ball.

Pro tip

How to troubleshoot Wegovy injection

If you find it difficult to inject, switch to a firmer injection site.

How to store and dispose of Wegovy?

How to store Wegovy injection pen?

  • Store the Wegovy injection pen in the refrigerator at a temperature between 2℃ and 8℃.
  • If you are in a hotter climate, you can store unused Wegovy injection pen at room temperature between 8℃ to 30℃ for up to 28 days. Keep the pen in its original packaging.
  • Keep away from direct sunlight.
  • Keep away from children and pets.
  • Do not freeze the injection pen.

How to dispose of Wegovy injection pen?

  • Do not dispose of your Wegovy injection pens in your household bin. Use a sharps bin container to dispose of the used injection pens.

Wegovy dosage schedule

Wegovy injection pen for weight loss comes in five different doses: 0.25 mg, 0.5 mg, 1 mg, 1.7 mg, and 2.4 mg. Doctors initially prescribe the lowest dose and then gradually increase it to maximise the medicine's efficacy in reducing body weight.

Here is what a typical Wegovy dosage schedule looks like:

Wegovy dosage schedule

Start

  • Start Wegovy at a dose of 0.25 mg weekly for the first month.

Step up

  • Increase your weekly dose to 0.5 mg in the second month.
  • Increase your weekly dose to 1 mg in the third month.
  • Increase your weekly dose to 1.7 mg in the fourth month.

Stay

  • Your doctor may make you stay at a maintenance dose of 1.7 mg at the start of the fifth month.
  • Your doctor may increase your Wegovy dosage to 2.4 mg at the start of the fifth month depending on whether you have met your weight loss goals or not.

What to do in case of a missed dose?

If you missed your Wegovy injection dose, do the following:

  • If your next dose is more than two days away from your missed dose, take the missed dose as soon as possible.
  • If your next dose is less than two days away from your missed dose, skip the missed dose and continue with your dosage schedule.
  • If you missed more than two consecutive doses, take your next dose as scheduled.
  • Do not take a double dose to make up for the missed dose.

Does Wegovy have any side effects?

Every medicine comes with a few side effects. Wegovy is no exception.

Some of the most common Wegovy side effects include:

  • Nausea
  • Stomach pain
  • Dizziness
  • Stomach flu
  • Diarrhoea
  • Headache
  • Bloating
  • Heartburn (acidity)
  • Vomiting
  • Fatigue
  • Runny nose/sore throat
  • Constipation
  • Upset stomach
  • Low blood sugar in type 2 diabetics
  • Gastric issues

Wegovy also has some serious but rare side effects. These include:

  • Pancreatitis (inflammation of the pancreas)
  • Gallbladder problems
  • Hypoglycemia (low blood sugar)
  • Kidney failure
  • Vision change in type 2 diabetics
  • Increased heart rate
  • Depression/suicidal thoughts
  • Allergic reactions (swelling of face, nose, lips)
  • Rash
  • Difficulty swallowing/breathing

These side effects usually subside within a few days to a few weeks. If they do not subside, contact your doctor immediately.

Safety advice to follow when taking Wegovy

When taking Wegovy, it's important to follow certain safety guidelines. This ensures the medication works effectively and side effects are minimised. Here's some key safety advice:

Category Safety Advice
Alcohol Use with caution Alcohol doesn't directly interact with Wegovy. But, it can affect your blood sugar levels. Drink alcohol in moderation and monitor your blood sugar closely.
Pregnancy Consult doctor Wegovy is not recommended during pregnancy. If you're pregnant or planning to become pregnant, discuss alternative treatments with your doctor.
Breastfeeding Consult doctor It's not known if Wegovy passes into breast milk. Discuss the potential risks and benefits with your doctor in this case.
Driving Use with caution Wegovy doesn't typically cause drowsiness. But if you experience dizziness or low blood sugar, avoid driving until you feel better.
Hydration Important Stay well-hydrated while taking Wegovy, especially if you experience diarrhoea or vomiting. This will help prevent dehydration.

What does Wegovy interact with?

Wegovy can interact with several other medications taken to treat other medical conditions. Inform your doctor if you are taking one or more medicines from the following list:

  • Insulin
  • Sulfonylureas (medicines taken to manage type 2 diabetes)
  • Oral medicines taken for various medical conditions

Is Wegovy available in India?

No, Wegovy is not currently available in India.

Karsten Mark Knudsen, CFO and EVP of Novo Nordisk, mentioned in an interview with Business Today that even though the company is “highly vested towards the Indian market,” it won’t be launching Wegovy in India this year. For Knudsen, the demand feels “overwhelming” and that the company “simply cannot manufacture enough compared to the demand in society.”

While the manufacturer hasn’t given an official timeline to launch Wegovy in India, it revealed to Reuters that its goal is to begin supply in India by 2026.

However, many pharmaceutical companies in India, such as Sun Pharma, Cipla, and Lupin are all working to develop different versions of Wegovy.

What does Wegovy cost in India?

Wegovy is not currently approved for sale in India. Of course, a grey market exists, where people who can pay large amounts of money can access Wegovy. Some doctors also prescribe the injection off-label.

However, the risks of using Wegovy off-label outweigh its benefits. Taking the injection for weight loss without a prescription or monitoring may land your health in trouble.

It is always better to stick to approved weight loss treatments, so that you can always be in control of your health.

FAQs

  • What should I do if my Wegovy injection pen isn’t working correctly?

    If your Wegovy injection pen isn’t working correctly, please contact your doctor immediately. You can also contact the manufacturer Novo Nordisk by visiting Wegovy.com.

  • Why are there different coloured Wegovy injection pens?

    Wegovy injection pens come in five different doses: 0.25 mg, 0.5 mg, 1 mg, 1.7 mg, and 2.4 mg. The pen has five different colours to denote the five different dosages.

  • Can I change the day or time to take Wegovy injection?

    It is recommended that you take the Wegovy injection on the same day and at the same time every week. However, if you would like to change the day and time when you take the Wegovy injection, you can do so two or more days after taking your last Wegovy dose.

  • Do I have to take Wegovy on an empty stomach?

    You can take the Wegovy injection on an empty stomach or after your meal. Both methods are fine. It all depends on your convenience.

  • I am nervous at the thought of injecting myself with Wegovy injection. What should I do?

    It is quite common to feel nervous before taking your Wegovy injection. To calm down, you can watch this video on taking a Wegovy injection or read these simple instructions before you begin.

    There is also a programme called ‘WeGo Together’ for Wegovy users. You can join the programme, where a coach will help you navigate the process of taking Wegovy.

  • Can I travel with Wegovy?

    Yes, you can travel with your Wegovy injection pen. However, keep the following things in mind:

    • If you are travelling by air, keep Wegovy in your carry-on luggage to avoid it from freezing in the baggage compartment.
    • If required, you can store the Wegovy injection pen in its original packaging at a temperature between 8℃ and 30℃.

  • How soon will I lose weight with Wegovy?

    Every individual is unique. And so is every individual’s weight loss journey. The time it takes for Wegovy to work differs from person to person. To get an accurate response to this question, it is best to contact your doctor, who will be able to guide you through this query.

  • Is Ozempic a substitute for Wegovy?

    Although Wegovy and Ozempic both contain semaglutide, they are different products with different uses and dosages as approved by the FDA. Each medication is safe and effective in its own way. As a result, they are not interchangeable.

  • Is there a generic version of Wegovy?

    Currently, there is no generic version of Wegovy available in the global market. This is because the manufacturer of Wegovy, Novo Nordisk, has patented the medicine until 2026. As a result, other pharmaceutical companies cannot produce or sell generic versions of Wegovy until that time.

  • If a generic version is available in the grey market, is it okay to use it instead of the actual Wegovy injection?

    Novo Nordisk, as the manufacturer of Wegovy, cannot validate the safety and efficacy of any generic version of the injection available in the grey market. As such, it is not advisable to risk your health by using generic or counterfeit medicines for weight loss.
