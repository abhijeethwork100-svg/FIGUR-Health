üíâTreatment
GLP-1 injections work by mimicking a naturally occurring gut hormone called glucagon-like peptide-1. When injected, they activate receptors in your brain and gut that signal satiety (fullness), slow gastric emptying, and reduce appetite. The result is that you feel full with less food and have significantly reduced cravings ‚Äî making it much easier to eat less and lose weight. The injections are subcutaneous (just under the skin) and come in easy-to-use auto-inject pens. Most people find them painless.
Rybelsus (semaglutide) is the first oral GLP-1 medication. It contains the same active ingredient as Ozempic and Wegovy injections, but in tablet form. It is taken once daily, at least 30 minutes before eating, with a small amount of water. While generally slightly less potent than the injectable versions, it is a good option for people who are uncomfortable with injections. Your doctor will advise on whether it's suitable for you.
As with all medications, GLP-1 drugs can have side effects. The most common are gastrointestinal and include: nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea, constipation, and stomach discomfort ‚Äî particularly in the early weeks of treatment. These typically reduce as your body adjusts, especially as the dose is increased gradually. More serious side effects are rare but can include pancreatitis, gallbladder problems, and changes in kidney function. Your doctor will discuss all potential side effects with you before prescribing, and our team monitors for adverse effects throughout your programme. If you experience any concerning symptoms, contact our medical team immediately.
Most people notice a reduction in appetite within 1‚Äì2 weeks of starting GLP-1 medication. Visible weight loss typically begins within 4‚Äì8 weeks. The medication works progressively ‚Äî as you gradually increase your dose over the first few months, the appetite-suppressing effects become stronger, and weight loss accelerates. Maximum results are typically seen at 12‚Äì16 months of continuous treatment at the optimal dose.
