What is GLP-1?

What are GLP-1 medications?

GLP-1 drugs refer to glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor agonists, also known as GLP-1 analogues or incretin mimetics. They mimic a naturally occurring gut hormone that plays a central role in regulating blood sugar and appetite.

The most advanced GLP-1 treatment available today is Mounjaro¬Æ (tirzepatide) ‚Äî a dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist that outperforms all previous weight loss medications in clinical trials. You may also have heard of Wegovy, Ozempic, or Rybelsus, which contain semaglutide ‚Äî an earlier-generation GLP-1 agonist that tirzepatide has now surpassed in head-to-head studies (SURMOUNT-5, NEJM 2025).

GLP-1 medicines work best long-term, in conjunction with exercise and a sensible diet. Over time, the dosage is gradually increased, giving your body plenty of time to adjust.

Originally developed to treat Type 2 diabetes, GLP-1 drugs were found to produce dramatic, consistent weight loss as a side effect ‚Äî leading to their development as dedicated weight loss treatments.

Is it scientifically proven?

The evidence is overwhelming

GLP-1 medications are among the most studied drugs in modern medicine. Multiple large-scale randomised controlled trials have consistently demonstrated their efficacy and safety.

The landmark SURMOUNT-1 trial showed tirzepatide (Mounjaro) produces a remarkable 22.5% average body weight reduction at the highest dose ‚Äî the largest weight loss ever recorded in a GLP-1 trial. In the head-to-head SURMOUNT-5 trial (NEJM 2025), tirzepatide delivered 47% more weight loss than semaglutide (Wegovy), reducing body weight by 20.2% vs 13.7%.

These drugs are approved by regulatory agencies worldwide, including the FDA (USA), EMA (Europe), and CDSCO (India), specifically for chronic weight management.
