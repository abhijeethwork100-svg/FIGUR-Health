What is Rybelsus?

Rybelsus is the oral form of semaglutide. It is prescribed for type 2 diabetes management. The FDA approved it in 2019. The Central Drugs Standard Control Organisation approved Rybelsus in India in 2022 for managing type 2 diabetes. It is currently available in three dosages: 3 mg, 7 mg, and 14 mg.

Even though Rybelsus is approved to manage type 2 diabetes, since it helps people lose weight simultaneously, doctors do sometimes prescribe it ‘off-label’ for weight loss as well.

How does Rybelsus work?

Semaglutide, which is the active ingredient in Rybelsus, is a GLP - 1 receptor agonist. Don’t let the phrase put you off. GLP-1 is a hormone that naturally occurs in our bodies.

This is what it does in our body:

How does GLP-1 work?
Releases insulin

GLP-1 hormone signals the pancreas to release insulin when blood sugar levels rise. This moves sugar into the cells to generate energy.

Slows digestion

The hormone slows down the pace of the movement of food through the stomach. This results in feeling fuller for longer after eating.

Reduces sugar production in the liver

The hormone decreases sugar production in the liver when it is not required.

Controls appetite

GLP-1 also affects the areas of the brain that control appetite. As a result, you end up feeling less hungry.

Semaglutide mimics this hormone in the body, eventually resulting in type 2 diabetes management and weight loss.

Rybelsus works exactly the same way as semaglutide - it makes the pancreas release more insulin, stops the liver from producing too much sugar, signals the brain to eat less, and slows down gastric emptying.

This simple function of the medicine has helped millions of people worldwide and in India become healthier versions of themselves within a year.

The growing popularity of Rybelsus for weight loss in India

In India, Rybelsus is proving to be a game-changer. The country currently has 7.7 crore type 2 diabetics. The number will only continue to rise.

As such, Rybelsus can turn the tables. The oral tablet can effectively control type 2 diabetes and help obese individuals reduce their weight in a non-invasive, safe, and sustainable manner.

In fact, Indians are already warming up to Rybelsus for better glucose control and weight management.

In an article published by the Economic Times in May 2024, Indian pharmaceutical analytics firm Pharmarack reported that Rybelsus sales in India ballooned from INR 47 crore a year in 2022 to INR 147 crore a year in 2023 to INR 363 crore in April 2024. Semaglutide currently holds a 66% share in the Indian anti-obesity market.

This does hold up, as 22% of the male population in the country is overweight, 23% of the female population is overweight, and 11% of children in this country are overweight as well.

Sheetal Sapale, Vice President - Commercial, Pharmarack, notes, “One in four adults falls in the overweight category. Almost 5% of the Indian population of 1.4 billion falls in the morbidly obese category.”

Thus, it comes as no surprise that Indians are now relying on Rybelsus to combat obesity and diabetes.

The question, however, still stands - Is Rybelsus really the anti-obesity and anti-diabetic drug that will work for you?

Who is eligible to take Rybelsus?

Rybelsus can be prescribed to:

  • People over 18 years old
  • Type 2 diabetics
  • Overweight or obese adults with a BMI of:
    • 30 kg/m2
    • 27 kg/m2 if one or more weight-related complications are present

Rybelsus isn't suitable for everyone. You may not be able to take it if you:

  • Have type 1 diabetes
  • Are allergic to semaglutide or any of the ingredients in Rybelsus
  • Have Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia Syndrome Type 2 (MEN 2)
  • Have Medullary Thyroid Carcinoma (MTC), which is a type of thyroid cancer
  • Are pregnant or breastfeeding
  • Have severe kidney problems or are on dialysis
  • Have a history of pancreatitis
  • Have severe gastrointestinal (stomach or intestine) problems, including gastroparesis (delayed stomach emptying)
  • Are under 18 years old

How to take Rybelsus?

It is important to take Rybelsus correctly. Follow this step-by-step guide to know more:

Key instructions
  • Take Rybelsus immediately after waking up.
  • Take it on an empty stomach. Wait for at least 30 minutes before eating/drinking anything or taking other oral tablets.
  • Take the whole tablet with half cup of water. Do not crush or chew the tablet.
  • If you miss a dose, skip it and continue with the regular dosage schedule. Do not take a double dose to make up for the missed dose.
Pro tip

Do you forget to take your medicines on time?

Set an alarm so that never miss taking your next Rybelsus dose.

How to store Rybelsus

Store Rybelsus at room temperature, between 20°C to 25°C.

Keep the medication in its original packaging. Do not expose it to direct sunlight and heat.

Keep out of reach of children and pets.

Pro tip

If you are travelling to a different climate, keep Rybelsus in an insulated medication travel bag to stabilise its temperature.

Rybelsus side effects

Now that you know when and how to take your Rybelsus tablet, it is also important to be aware about Rybelsus side effects.

Common side effects:

  • Nausea
  • Diarrhea
  • Vomiting
  • Stomach (abdominal) pain
  • Constipation
  • Headache
  • Fatigue
  • Decreased appetite

Less common side effects:

  • Dizziness
  • Gas
  • Stomach flu
  • Heartburn
  • Belching
  • Hair loss

Rare but serious side effects:

  • Pancreatitis (inflammation of the pancreas)
  • Changes in vision
  • Low blood sugar (hypoglycemia), especially if you're also taking other diabetes medications
  • Kidney problems
  • Allergic reactions

If you experience any of these side effects, contact your doctor right away. Refer to the to know more about Rybelsus side effects.

If the side effects get too severe and you require immediate medical attention, please call 108 to get quick ambulance services and seek medical care without further delay.

Tips to handle nausea while on Rybelsus

Nausea can feel unsettling. But when you are on Rybelsus, there are a few things you can do to reduce nausea or keep it at bay:

  • Eat non-spicy, low-fat foods like biscuits, toast, or rice.
  • Eat liquid foods, such as soups or natural juices.
  • Stay away from deep-fried, greasy foods.
  • Do not lie down immediately after eating your meals.
  • Take a small outdoor walk. Breathe fresh air.
  • Eat small meals. Eat slowly.
  • Drink clear water. Add some ice cubes in it if you like it chilled.

Safety advice to follow when taking Rybelsus

When taking Rybelsus, it's important to follow certain safety guidelines. This ensures the medication works effectively and side effects are minimised. Here's some key safety advice:

Category Safety Advice
Alcohol Use with caution Alcohol doesn't interact with Rybelsus. But, it may make your blood sugar levels fluctuate. Drink alcohol in moderate amounts and keep an eye on your blood sugar levels.
Pregnancy Consult doctor Rybelsus is not safe for pregnant women. If you're pregnant or planning to conceive, discuss alternative treatments with your doctor.
Breastfeeding Consult doctor It's not known if Rybelsus mixes with breast milk. Discuss the risks and benefits of taking Rybelsus during breastfeeding with your doctor in this case.
Driving Use with caution Rybelsus doesn't usually make patients drowsy. But if you feel dizzy or notice low blood sugar levels, avoid driving until you feel better.
Hydration Important Stay well-hydrated while taking Rybelsus, especially if you are going through diarrhoea or feel like vomiting. This will help prevent dehydration.

Drug interactions

Rybelsus may interact with other medicines you are currently taking. Before you start taking Rybelsus, let your doctor know if you are taking one or more of the medicines mentioned below:

  • Rybelsus may increase the risk of low blood sugar when taken with insulin or sulfonylureas, such as glipizide or glyburide.
  • Rybelsus affects how quickly your body absorbs other oral medications. To avoid this, take Rybelsus at least 30 minutes before other oral medications.
  • The oral tablet may increase the blood-thinning effects of blood thinners like Warfarin.
  • The medicine may reduce the effectiveness of oral contraceptives.
  • Rybelsus may affect thyroid hormone levels. So, you might need to adjust the dose of thyroid medications.

FAQs

  • How soon can I expect Rybelsus to start working?

    Rybelsus starts lowering blood sugar levels within 1-2 weeks of starting treatment. However, it may take 4-5 weeks (sometimes even longer) to see its full effect on blood sugar levels and weight loss. Remember, weight loss results may vary based on other factors like your diet, exercise routine, and overall lifestyle.

  • How is Rybelsus different from Ozempic?

    Rybelsus and Ozempic both contain the same active ingredient, semaglutide. Both medicines help manage type 2 diabetes. However, the difference lies in how they are administered. Rybelsus is taken as an oral tablet, while Ozempic is taken as a once-weekly injection under the skin.

  • Why can’t Rybelsus be used for type 1 diabetes?

    Rybelsus isn’t suitable for type 1 diabetes because the medicine helps the body make more insulin. The tablet only works if your pancreas still has some cells that can produce insulin, which is not the case in type 1 diabetes. Type 1 diabetics need insulin injections rather than tablets because their body cannot make insulin at all.

  • Does Rybelsus work differently for people of different ethnic backgrounds?

    Rybelsus has been tested on people from various ethnic backgrounds. Studies show it works similarly across different groups. However, individual responses can vary due to genetics, lifestyle, or other factors. It’s important to follow doctor’s advice based on your specific health needs to meet your weight loss goals with Rybelsus.

  • What should I do if I don’t see results after starting Rybelsus?

    If you don’t see results after taking Rybelsus for several weeks or months, consult your doctor. They might adjust your dose, recommend lifestyle changes like diet or exercise, or suggest alternative treatment options. Don’t make changes to your medication without informing your doctor.

  • Can I stop taking Rybelsus once my blood sugar is controlled or I’ve lost enough weight?

    Rybelsus helps manage type 2 diabetes and aids in weight loss, but these are ongoing conditions. Stopping the medication suddenly can cause your blood sugar spikes and may make you gain weight. Always talk to your doctor before making any changes to your treatment.

  • Can I take a generic version of Rybelsus instead?

    Currently, a generic version of Rybelsus is not available because the medication is protected by patents. The tablet form of semaglutide is unique. Hence, generics cannot be made until the patent expires.

  • Is Rybelsus available without a prescription?

    No, Rybelsus is a prescription-only medication. You need a valid prescription from a licensed doctor to purchase it from both local and online pharmacies.

  • Can I buy Rybelsus online?

    Yes, you can buy Rybelsus from licensed online pharmacies, but you must have a prescription from a doctor. Always ensure the online pharmacy is licensed and reliable so that you don’t receive counterfeit products.

  • How can I ensure I’m getting genuine Rybelsus from my pharmacy?

    To make sure you’re getting genuine Rybelsus, buy only from licensed pharmacies. The packaging must have a clear labeling, a batch number, and an expiration date. It should also be sealed and intact. If you have any doubts or questions, consult your doctor or contact the manufacturer directly.
